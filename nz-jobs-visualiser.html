Digital AI Exposure (0–10) estimates how much current AI will reshape each occupation. Calibrated using the RBNZ Analytical Note (Feb 2026): "Assessing AI and Robotics Exposure in the NZ Labour Market Using LLMs" — which found that AI exposure is highest in professional, managerial, and administrative occupations and in middle-to-upper income brackets ($50k–$150k). Machinery operators, labourers, and community workers show significantly lower AI exposure. Individual occupation scores here apply the Karpathy/BLS digital AI exposure methodology (0–10 scale) calibrated to NZ Census ANZSCO occupations.

Key signal: Jobs done entirely on a computer (writing, coding, analysing, communicating) score 7–10. Jobs requiring physical presence, manual dexterity, or real-time human interaction in unpredictable environments score 1–3. Scores in the 4–6 range indicate a meaningful mix of both.

⚠ A high score predicts restructuring, not necessarily replacement. Software engineers score 9/10 because AI is transforming their work — but demand for software could grow as each engineer becomes more productive. The score does not account for demand elasticity, latent demand, regulatory barriers, or social preferences for human workers.
